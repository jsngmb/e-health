What is Mental Health?

Mental health is a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act. It also helps determine how we handle stress, make decisions, build relationships, and cope with challenges in everyday life. Good mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood to adulthood.

Learn more on WHO.int →

Why is Mental Health Important?

Helps people manage stress and emotions.
Improves relationships with family and friends.
Increases focus, learning, and productivity.
Supports physical health and overall well-being.
Encourages confidence and positive decision-making.
Reference: WHO.int →

Common Mental Health Conditions

Anxiety Disorders

Constant worry, fear, or nervousness.

Depression

Persistent sadness, loss of interest, low energy.

Stress-Related

Emotional and physical effects of overwhelming stress.

Bipolar Disorder

Extreme mood changes between highs and lows.

Learn more about these conditions →

Signs That Someone May Need Help

  • Feeling sad or anxious for a long time.
  • Changes in sleeping or eating habits.
  • Loss of interest in favorite activities.
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions.
  • Feeling hopeless or overwhelmed.
  • Withdrawing from family and friends.
Reference: NIMH.gov →

Tips for Maintaining Good Mental Health

  • 1 Get enough sleep every night.
  • 2 Eat healthy and stay hydrated.
  • 3 Exercise regularly.
  • 4 Spend time with supportive people.
  • 5 Practice relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or meditation.
  • 6 Take breaks from school, work, and social media.
  • 7 Ask for help from trusted friends, family members, or a mental health professional when needed.

Breaking the Stigma

Mental health problems are common and treatable. Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness.

By showing kindness, listening without judgment, and supporting one another, we can help create a community where everyone feels safe to ask for help.
Reference: WHO.int →

"There is no health without mental health."

Taking care of your mind is just as important as taking care of your body.
